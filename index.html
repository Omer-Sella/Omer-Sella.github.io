About

My work is around taking concepts from theoretical information and coding theory and applying them to real world problems. It starts with understanding the underlying physical system (quartz glass, DNA, opto-electrical modules...) and asking what mathematical model best describes the system and its noise. Then there is a process of understanding which solutions / coding methods / algorithms can be useful and what are the constraints that will dictate the specific choice and implementation. This process is only as good as my understanding of the underlying problem and the desired outcome, and I have been very fortunate to work with (and be part of) many and varied teams, that often taught me a lot !.

More recently, I shifted my focus to quantum error correction. I maintain a (growing, educational) repository that tries to introduce QECC without going through quantum algorithms first (as I find most books do).

Research

Current Focus

Quantum Error Correction

We are in a super exciting time for Quantum LDPC codes! My work takes classical decoders for Error Correcting Codes and adapts them to the stabilizer formalism. I try to document my journey so that other experts in classical ECC could use my tools and intuition to make their own transition to the quantum setting.

github.com/Omer-Sella/qecc ↗
Deep Reinforcement Learning

Learning LDPC Codes

Formulating the search for good Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes as a Markov decision process — consecutive replacement of circulant sub-matrices — and using Proximal Policy Optimization to discover novel parity-check matrices that outperform handcrafted constructions.

github.com/Omer-Sella/ldpc ↗
Message Passing

Belief Propagation Decoding

LDPC codes are decoded via iterative message-passing on their Tanner graph — variable nodes and check nodes exchange soft-decision messages until the codeword converges. Understanding and accelerating this process is central to both classical and quantum LDPC work.

Archival Storage

DNA Data Storage

Revisiting over two decades of Molecular Biology and Bioinformatics with data storage in mind. Designing encodings that avoid problematic sequences (homopolymers, low GC-content), reduce synthesis cost, and recover gracefully from insertion-deletion errors.
